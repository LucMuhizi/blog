Our values are not decorative. They shape what we take on, who we work with, and what we are willing to say no to.

## Rigour

We treat evidence seriously. We do not paraphrase a report we have not read, claim a finding we cannot trace, or borrow a framework we cannot interrogate. We name our uncertainties.

## Future-orientation

We work for the long arc. Short-term compromise is sometimes necessary; long-term compromise is not. Our research, policy, and convening work is calibrated to outcomes measured in decades, not news cycles.

## Intellectual independence

We are not captured by funder, government, or movement politics in ways that distort our analysis. Where conflicts of interest exist, we name them.

## Structural equity

Equality of opportunity is necessary but not sufficient. We work on the design of institutions, policies, and systems — the architecture — because that is where durable change happens.

## Gender equality

Gender is the most reliable predictor of who carries the cost of structural failure. We refuse to disaggregate equity from gender, or gender from the rest of the structural analysis.

## Courage without recklessness

We name what is true even when it is uncomfortable. We do not mistake provocation for analysis.

## What we will not do

- We will not lend our name to work that erases the lived experience of marginalised people.
- We will not take engagements where the institutional ask is incompatible with the evidence.
- We will not treat rigour, rigour-shaped, as a posture. It is a discipline.
